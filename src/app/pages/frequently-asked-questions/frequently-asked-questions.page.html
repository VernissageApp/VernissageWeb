Frequently Asked Questions

Welcome to the Vernissage FAQ - a curated space to help you get the most out of your photo sharing experience.

1. Does Vernissage support keyboard shortcuts?

Yes. Vernissage offers extensive keyboard navigation for users to help you move quickly between pages, timelines, and actions.

Global Shortcuts (available throughout the app, for logged-in users):

  • H - Go to the Home timeline
  • T - Open the Trending timeline
  • E - Open the Editor's Choice timeline
  • C - View Categories
  • N - View News
  • U - Upload a new photo
  • P - Go to your Profile
  • F - Open this FAQ page

If the current page includes multiple tabs (e.g. Home, Trending, or Editor's Choice), you can switch between them by pressing the number keys:

  • 1 - Switch to the first tab
  • 2 - Switch to the second tab
  • 3 - Switch to the third tab

On a Status Detail Page:

  • ← - View the next status
  • → - View the previous status
  • L - Like the status
  • B - Boost the status
  • S - Save the status to bookmarks

These shortcuts are designed to enhance accessibility and speed up your browsing - especially helpful for keyboard-centric users.

2. What's the recommended resolution for uploaded images?

Vernissage does not restrict image resolution - only file size matters. You're free to upload high-resolution images as long as the file remains within the allowed upload size.

However, to ensure optimal quality and compatibility across the Fediverse, we recommend uploading images in up to 4K resolution (approximately 3840 x 2160 pixels). This strikes a balance between image clarity and performance and is fully supported across platforms like Mastodon and Pixelfed.

Note:

  • Mastodon may display the image as a placeholder link instead of rendering it inline.
  • Pixelfed may reject the image entirely if it exceeds 5000px on any side.

For the best visibility and interoperability, keep it 4K or below.
